CORTICOLIMBIC CIRCUIT
NEXT>
The Corticolimbic Circuit is a brain circuit
that is mainly responsible for behavioral alertness.
NEXT>
The amydala is the main brain region of this brain circuit.
NEXT>
The amydala is a region that responds to perceived
negative stimuli and is associated with our body's fear response.
NEXT>
The amygdala alerts the body when it detects
potential danger, also bringing up stress and anxiety levels.
Consequently, the heart rate increases.
NEXT>
Once the perceived threat goes away, the Prefrontal Cortex
will be activated to work as an off switch for the amygdala,
bringing our physiological responses back to baseline.
NEXT>
This healthy feedback loop between the amygdala and
Prefrontal Cortex, however, is absent in depressed individuals.
NEXT>
Those with depression have a much more sensitive
and hyperactive amygdala response when exposed to perceived threat.
< BACK TO FIRST PAGE
In fact, the amydala in depressed brains seem to respond
even to neutral stimuli, showing how depressed individuals often
misperceive cues in negative ways.
