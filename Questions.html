Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What if I have never been camping?

Every year we have participants that have never been camping before. Afterall, you've gotta have a first. Use the Wilderness Project as an opportunity to explore a potential new interest. We will teach you all the basic skills needed for the trip.

Q: What if I'm on medication?

A:Please bring all the medication you need to the trip. Make sure to list all medications on the application so we are aware of your medical conditions. Please bring all medication that you need to the trip and inform the project leaders about any potential medical situations including allergic reactions.

Q: What if I have special dietary needs?

Contact us with your specific dietary needs and we will try our best to accomodate.

Q: Why does the Wilderness Project cost more than other Pre-Orientation Programs?

The program restration fee helps to cover a variety of expenses including food, leader trainings, gear acquisition, and transportation from the airport to campus and from campus to the woods.

Q: What about Financial Aid?

The Wilderness Project is dedicated to providng experiential education and outdoor recreation regardless of financial means. We offer two need-based scholarships. Please contact us if the program cost is prohibitive.

Q: When can I move in to my room?

You can move in the day we return to campus (August 21st). The dorms will not be available for move-in before. You should arrange so that your belongings of your belongings besides your camping gear should be shipped or driven in to arrive on the 21st.

Q: How can my parents reach me if there is an emergency at home while I am on the trail?

Parents can call the Orientation Office from 9:00 am to 5:00 pm, Monday through Friday at (314) 935-8350. After-hours emergency contact information will be distributed to parents before freshman arrive on campus.

If you have any more questions, please contact us at wildernessproject@gmail.com.
