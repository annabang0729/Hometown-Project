CORTICOSTRIATAL CIRCUIT
NEXT>
The Corticostriatal Circuit is a brain circuit
that is mainly responsible for motivational behaviors.
NEXT>
The ventral striatum is the main brain region of this brain circuit.
NEXT>
Through the ventral striatum, people find motivation to
act upon reward-predicting signals such as entertainment,
food, and money.
NEXT>
For instance, when one is hungry, the ventral striatum
shows strong activation to the sight of pizza.
NEXT>
We then feel motivated as the ventral striatum communicates
to other appropriate brain regions that coordinate a plan to obtain pizza.

Those with depression generally show a weaker
ventral striatum response to reward signals. This explains
the symptoms of withdrawal and the overall lack of drive in
daily activities that are most common in depression.
